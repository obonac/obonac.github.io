## My revised assessment

When we started, I thought this was a biography.

Now I think that's the wrong genre.

The closest analogue is something like:

* Donald Knuth's *The Art of Computer Programming* (multi-volume technical work),
* the collected works of a research laboratory,
* a software architecture reference,
* and an intellectual history,

combined into one interconnected corpus.

It would not be read front-to-back like a memoir.

It would function as a **reference work**: readers could enter through enterprise architecture, multilingual computing, educational theory, healthcare systems, AI infrastructure, or programming languages, and navigate the cross-links. The "book" is really a **knowledge atlas** documenting the evolution of a long-running multidisciplinary research programme.
